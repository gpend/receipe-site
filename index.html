Entrees

Crock-pot spaghetti

  • 1 large green pepper, chopped
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 clove garlic (or a whole head, your choice :)
  • 1.5 lbs ground beef
  • 1 t salt (optional)
  • red and/or black pepper to taste
  • 8 oz spaghetti
  • 1 c grated carrots
  • 1 No. 2 can (~ 20oz) Tomatoes
  • 1 No. 2 can (~ 20oz) Water
  • 1 small can tomato paste
  • 1 T Oregano
  • Dash of catsup
  • Dash of Worcestershire sauce

Brown pepper, onion, and garlic in oil in medium skillet, when onions are translucent, add ground beef. Cook beef and chop into small bits.
Add remaining ingredients and bring to Boil ("High" setting on crock pot), then Simmer - 3 hours
Add 8 oz spaghetti, Cook on "Low" setting of crock pot.
Makes lots, can be frozen, do not overcook spaghetti.

Deserts

Cinnamon rolls

Roll dough

  • 1 pk active dry yeast
  • ¾ tsp warm water
  • 1 cup milk, scalded
  • ½ cup sugar
  • ¾ cup shortening
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg, room temp

Filling

  • 1 Tbs Cinnamon
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • ¼ cup firmly packed brown sugar
  • ¼ ?stick? softened butter

Makes ½ sheet pan full, 2X for crowd
Soften yeast in warm water.
Combine milk, sugar, shortening, and salt; cool to lukewarm.
Add 1½ cups of flour; beat well. Beat in yeast and egg. Gradually add remaining flour to form soft dough, beating well.
Place in greased bowl, turning once to grease surface.
Cover with plastic wrap and let rise till double (1½ - 2 hours)
Turn out on lightly floured surface and shape as desired – for cinnamon rolls, mix cinnamon and sugars together, roll dough to rectangle approximately ¼ -½” thick, spread butter on rolled out dough, sprinkle cinnamon and sugar mixture evenly.
Roll up dough and cut log at 1" intervals.
Cover and let shaped rolls rise till double (30 to 45 minutes).
Bake on greased baking sheet in hot oven (400◦F) for 12 to 15 minutes.
Frosting may be added after cooling somewhat – 2 cups confectioner sugar stirred into 2 tablespoons milk, OJ to get desired pouring consistency. Almond or vanilla may be added to taste... Flour quantity may vary according to temp and humidity.
Warm = 110-115◦F. Oven = 400◦F

Peanut butter balls

  • 3.75 Cups corn flakes
  • 0.75 Cups peanut butter
  • 0.75 Cups brown sugar
  • 0.375 Cup dark corn syrup

Heat the brown sugar and syrup to boiling. Remove from heat and stir in peanut butter.
Pour this mixture over the cornflakes. Mix gently
Form into balls
Flake coconut can be added to corn flakes before forming into balls.
